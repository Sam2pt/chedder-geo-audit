# Chedder launch campaign brief

What we're launching, to whom, with what message, in what sequence.
Every design and caption gets checked against this. If a post doesn't
clearly serve one of the four jobs below, it doesn't ship.

Last updated: 2026-06-29

---

## 1. The job

This is the **first time the market hears Chedder exists.**

Single goal of the launch sequence: get a target buyer to run their
free audit. Not click through. Not subscribe to anything. Not book a
demo. **Run their first audit.** That's the conversion event.

Everything downstream (Pro upgrades, retention, word of mouth)
depends on getting them to the activation moment, which is the
audit completing and showing them where they stand.

---

## 2. Who we're for

### Primary buyer

A DTC or CPG brand marketer — title varies (Founder, Head of Growth,
Marketing Director, Brand Manager) — who has:

* Watched organic traffic decline in the last 12-18 months
* Heard "AI search" / "GEO" / "AEO" enough times to be curious
* Not yet found a tool that's actually built for their category
* 10-15 minutes per day on LinkedIn, in evenings

What they care about:
* Their brand showing up where shoppers are looking
* Concrete actions they can ship without engineering
* Not getting embarrassed by their CEO asking "what's our AI score?"

What they don't care about:
* "Generative engine optimization" as a technical concept
* SEO theory dressed up in new acronyms
* Anything that requires a 30-minute demo to understand

### Where they are

LinkedIn is the primary channel. They scroll their feed Sunday
evening and Wednesday lunchtime. They engage with:

* Founder-voice posts > company-voice posts
* Single-stat hooks > multi-paragraph essays
* Visuals with clear data > visuals with vague metaphor
* "Here's what we found" > "Here's what we built"

---

## 3. The hook

The one thing this whole campaign is selling:

> Your AI search problem can be solved in 60 seconds.

Three elements, every asset carries at least two:

| Element | What it does |
|---|---|
| **60 seconds** | Defuses time anxiety. No commitment. |
| **47 signals checked** | Proves seriousness. Not a vibe check. |
| **Free, no signup** | Removes the friction that kills 90% of try-it intent. |

Avoid: "comprehensive analysis", "complete audit", "deep dive". These
read as long, slow, and expensive — the exact wrong signal.

---

## 4. The four-post arc

Four posts over 14 days. Each does ONE job. If a post is doing two
jobs, it's two posts.

### Day 0 · Post 1 · INTRODUCE

**Job:** Make the buyer aware Chedder exists and what it is in 1
sentence.

**Visual:** Chedder character as the hero (220-300px). Headline:
"Meet Chedder. Your AI search assistant." Coral on "Chedder". URL
plain text bottom-left. Background: cream.

**Caption (founder voice):** Hook line = the product in 8 words.
Three lines of context. URL. Nothing else.

> Today we're launching Chedder.
>
> It checks 47 signals across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Brave, and Google
> AI in 60 seconds, then tells you exactly what's keeping your brand
> out of AI answers. Free for your first one, no signup.
>
> Built for DTC and CPG brands. chedder.2pt.ai

**Success:** Comment-to-impression ratio above 1%. Direct site
visits in the 24h window.

---

### Day 3 · Post 2 · THE STAT THAT EARNS ATTENTION

**Job:** Validate the buyer's suspicion that something is wrong.
Stat-led so it spreads.

**Visual:** Display XL stat ("50%") in coral. Tiny eyebrow above.
One-line statement below. Chedder small (80-120px) in thinking
variant, bottom-right. Background: cream.

**Caption:**

> Across the first 200 brands Chedder audited, 50% are blocking
> GPTBot. By accident.
>
> Most picked the block up from 2023 advice that aged badly. They're
> now invisible to ChatGPT while their competitors get cited daily.
> One line in robots.txt fixes it.
>
> Find out what's blocking yours: chedder.2pt.ai

**Success:** Reshares. The stat travels.

---

### Day 7 · Post 3 · THE 60-SECOND PROMISE

**Job:** Crystallize the hook. Single-image proof of speed.

**Visual:** Display XL "60" or "60s" in white, on coral background.
Sub: "URL in. Action plan out." Chedder small in celebrating
variant, peeking. URL bottom-left. Background: coral.

**Caption:**

> Sixty seconds. From the moment you paste your URL to the moment
> you're reading the action plan.
>
> No signup. No demo call. No "let me check your industry first."
> Just an answer.
>
> chedder.2pt.ai

**Success:** Site visits. This is the closer post — the one that
sends a buyer to the audit form.

### Day 12 · Post 4 · THE PROOF

**Job:** Show what they'll actually get. Visual proof of substance.

**Visual:** The audit dashboard mock (single card, not the tilted

Chedder small, top-right, idle variant. Background: slate.

**Caption:**

**Success:** Conversion (audits run). This post catches the buyer
who needed proof before clicking.

## 5. What we're NOT doing in the launch

* **No carousel posts.** Saves a 10-slide breakdown for later, when
  there's actual case-study data to share.
* **No video posts.** The ChedderLoop animation exists for later
  pinning to the company page or paid ads. Save it.
* **No founder hot-takes about the AI search category.** That's
  thought-leadership content for month 2-3, after the product is
  introduced. Don't dilute the launch.
* **No discount or promo.** Free first audit is the entire offer.
  Anything cheaper than free reads as desperate.
* **No multi-CTA posts.** "Run the audit OR book a demo OR sign up
  for the newsletter" splits the energy. One ask.

---

## 6. Voice for the launch

Per the brand system:

* **Posts 1, 3:** Brand voice. Confident, direct, restrained.
* **Posts 2, 4:** Half founder voice ("Across the first 200 brands
  Chedder audited..."), half observational ("This is what 60 seconds
  looks like..."). Founder voice gets the higher engagement because
  LinkedIn rewards human-shaped posts over company-page-shaped posts.

All four posts can be cross-posted from the Chedder company page AND
reposted by the founder personally. The founder repost should
include a short personal hook ("Built this because..." or "Honestly
surprised by what we found...").

---

## 7. Channel rules

### LinkedIn

* Post around **8-10am ET Tuesday-Thursday** (when DTC/CPG marketers
  scroll most actively).
* Skip Friday-Sunday for launches. Reposts/threads can go any day.
* All four posts get cross-posted from company page AND founder.
  Founder post hits 5-10x the company page on LinkedIn.

### Where we're not launching (yet)

* Twitter/X — secondary channel, can mirror the LinkedIn posts but
  not part of the launch sequence
* Email — no list yet
* Product Hunt — save for month 2 (PH launches need a coordinated
  push and the launch sequence here is the warmup, not the event)
* Paid — no paid until the organic post engagement tells us which
  hook resonates. Then promote the winner.

## 8. Measurement

### Launch week (day 0-7)

| Metric | Watching for |
|---|---|
| LinkedIn impressions | 50K+ across the four posts (across founder + company page) |
| Comment-to-impression rate | 1%+ on at least one post |
| Site sessions from LinkedIn | 800+ in launch week |
| Audits run | 200+ in launch week |
| Audits → Pro upgrade | 1-3 conversions in launch week (early signal, not statistically meaningful) |

### Launch month (day 0-30)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Cumulative audits run | 1,000+ |
| Branded search lift on chedder.2pt.ai | Detectable in Google Search Console |
| Inbound LinkedIn DMs | 5-10 (qualitative — who's reaching out?) |

What we DON'T measure in launch:
* Revenue (too early — Pro conversion lags introduction by weeks)
* MRR (same reason)
* CAC (no paid spend yet)

## 9. After the launch

If the launch lands, the next 30 days look like:

* **Editorial posts** — start the cadence of 1-2 posts per week
  using the existing blog content as the source material.
* **Programmatic brand pages** — promote a handful of the 197 brand
  detail pages as standalone LinkedIn posts ("Saatva vs Casper vs
  Purple in AI search — here's the leaderboard").
* **Customer-driven content** — once any audited brand publicly
  references Chedder, repost it with permission. User-driven posts
  outperform brand-driven 5:1.
* **First Product Hunt push** — month 2, with a coordinated launch
  day.

If the launch misses, the next 30 days look like:

* **One hook is wrong.** Re-test the 60-seconds-vs-47-signals-vs-
  free triangle. Pick a different lead element.
* **Wrong channel mix.** Try the same posts on Twitter / X, on
  Indie Hackers, on a couple of relevant Slack communities.
* **Product gap.** If everyone who tries an audit bounces, the
  audit isn't delivering the promise. Don't make more posts — fix
  the audit.

---

## 10. The single sentence that makes this brief unambiguous

If you're designing a post and you're not sure whether it belongs in
the launch, ask: "Does this make a DTC marketer paste their URL into
chedder.2pt.ai today?"

If yes, ship it. If no, save it for later.
