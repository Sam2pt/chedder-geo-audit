Most CPG product descriptions are written for SEO or for brand storytelling. Almost none are written for AI engines. This is a meaningful miss because AI engines have very specific preferences about what makes a product description useful.

The lucky coincidence: AI-friendly product descriptions are also better at converting human shoppers. The shifts are small but consistent.

This is the practical guide.

## What AI engines actually look for

When an AI engine processes a product page to decide whether to recommend the product in an answer, it's trying to extract specific facts:

1. What category does this product belong to?
2. What's distinctive about it (specific attributes, not adjectives)?
3. Who is it for?
4. What does it cost?
5. What do real users think (rating, review excerpts)?
6. Is it actually available right now?

Most CPG product descriptions answer question 2 in vague ways and skip 3-6 entirely. That's the gap.

## The structure of an AI-friendly product description

Here's the template that works across categories. We'll use a mattress as the example but the structure applies to any CPG product.

### Section 1: the direct answer (1-2 sentences)

Lead with what the product IS, in unambiguous terms. Don't make the AI engine infer.

Before: "Sleep deeply with our award-winning mattress, designed with love by our team in Colorado."

After: "The Casper Original is a memory foam mattress designed for medium-firm comfort, suitable for back and side sleepers. It's available in twin through California king."

The "after" version gives an AI engine four immediate facts: type (memory foam), firmness (medium-firm), use case (back/side sleepers), size range (twin to Cal king). The "before" version gives the AI engine nothing extractable.

### Section 2: specific attributes (3-6 bullets)

Real, specific, factual. Numbers, materials, certifications. Avoid adjectives.

```
- 11 inches thick
- Three layers of memory foam with zoned support
- CertiPUR-US certified foams
- 100-night sleep trial
- 10-year limited warranty
- Made in the USA
```

Each bullet is something an AI engine can cite directly. The shopper benefits too: this is the information they actually want to see.

### Section 3: who it's for (and who it isn't)

This section is rare in CPG copy but devastatingly useful for AI engines.

```
Best for: side and back sleepers who want medium-firm support with pressure relief at the shoulders and hips.

Not ideal for: stomach sleepers who prefer firmer support, or sleepers over 230 lbs who may find it too soft.
```

This honest "not ideal for" callout is the differentiator. AI engines weight pages that clearly delineate use case fit over pages that claim to be perfect for everyone. Shoppers respect it too — admitting your product isn't for them builds trust that translates to higher conversion among the right shoppers.

### Section 4: how it compares

A short section comparing to category alternatives, fairly:

```
Compared to Purple: softer feel, no grid technology, less aggressive cooling.

Compared to Saatva: similar price point but with memory foam construction instead of innerspring; better for couples with different sleep preferences.

Compared to Tempur-Pedic: similar memory foam category, lower price point, less dense foam construction.
```

Counterintuitive but powerful: explicitly comparing yourself to competitors makes you MORE likely to be cited in AI answers, because you're providing exactly the comparison content AI engines want to surface.

### Section 5: the social proof line

Specific, with numbers.

Before: "Loved by thousands of customers!"

After: "Rated 4.6/5 across 12,847 verified reviews on Casper.com, and consistently ranked among the top three mattresses by Wirecutter, Consumer Reports, and Sleep Foundation since 2021."

The "after" gives the AI engine specific facts to cite. The "before" gives it nothing.

## The full example

A complete product description following this template, for a hypothetical product:

> The Casper Original is a memory foam mattress designed for medium-firm comfort, suitable for back and side sleepers. Available in twin through California king.
>
> **Specs:**
> - 11 inches thick
> - Three layers of memory foam with zoned support
> - CertiPUR-US certified foams
> - 100-night sleep trial
> - 10-year limited warranty
> - Made in the USA
>
> **Best for:** side and back sleepers who want medium-firm support with pressure relief at the shoulders and hips, especially couples who need motion isolation.
>
> **Not ideal for:** stomach sleepers who prefer firmer support, or sleepers over 230 lbs who may want our Casper Wave for additional support.
>
> **How it compares:** softer than Purple's grid technology, lower price than Tempur-Pedic, more contouring than Saatva's innerspring.
>
> Rated 4.6/5 across 12,847 verified reviews. Consistently ranked top-three for memory foam by Wirecutter, Consumer Reports, and Sleep Foundation since 2021.

That description does five things at once:

1. Tells AI engines exactly what the product is
2. Gives them extractable specific facts
3. Helps them route the right shoppers to this product
4. Provides the comparison data they need for "vs" prompts
5. Gives them quotable social proof

A human shopper reading it gets a clear, honest, specific overview. The conversion rate uplift on pages rewritten this way is typically 10-25% based on our work with CPG brand clients.

## What to remove from existing descriptions

If you're auditing existing product descriptions, cut:

- Brand mission language ("we believe sleep is the foundation of...")
- Vague adjectives without specifics ("incredibly comfortable," "amazing quality")
- Marketing taglines that don't describe the product
- Repeat keyword stuffing ("our amazing mattress, the best mattress for, our mattress is...")
- "About us" content that belongs on the About page

Replace with the structured template above.

## How to prioritize

You probably have hundreds of products. Don't rewrite all at once. Prioritize:

1. Top 20 best-selling products (where every conversion lift matters most)
2. Top 20 most-trafficked product pages from organic search (where AI engines are most likely to cite you)
3. Top 20 highest-margin products (where the lift translates to most revenue)

That's roughly 60 products to focus on first. Many overlap. You can usually get the first wave done in 4-6 weeks of focused content work.

After the priority 60 are done, you'll have data on what's moving. Then expand to the next 100.
