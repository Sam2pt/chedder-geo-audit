A common 2026 conversation: a CPG brand marketer pulls up their Google Search Console and sees organic traffic down 15-30% year-over-year despite stable or improving keyword rankings. The pages rank the same. The clicks don't come.

This is almost always Google AI Overviews. When the user sees an AI-generated answer at the top of the search results page that contains the information they were looking for, they don't click through to your site. The position-1 ranking that used to drive 30% of clicks now drives 5-8%.

This post is the practical recovery playbook: what's happening, why it's happening, and what to do about it.

## The structural shift

Google's AI Overviews launched broadly in mid-2024 and now appear on roughly 50% of commercial search queries in the US. The trend line is still going up. By the end of 2026, most analysts expect Overviews on 70-80% of commercial queries.

For information-intent queries ("what is muslin gauze"), Overviews are devastating to click-through rates. The user gets their answer and leaves. The page that used to capture that traffic doesn't.

For shopping-intent queries ("best mattress for back pain"), Overviews shift WHICH pages get clicks rather than eliminating clicks entirely. The Overview names specific brands and provides citation links. Brands cited in the Overview get clicks. Brands ranking on the SERP below the Overview lose them.

## Three patterns in the traffic loss

When we dig into the traffic decline pattern at brand-level audits, three subpatterns explain most cases:

### Pattern 1: education content that no longer drives clicks

If your blog includes posts like "what is collagen" or "how to wash silk pajamas," these are exactly the queries Overviews now answer directly. Your post still ranks but gets ~5% the clicks it used to.

This is the largest single bucket of traffic loss for most CPG brands. Education content built the long-tail empire of the 2010s and is bleeding traffic in 2026.

### Pattern 2: review and comparison content that lost to citations

"Best [category]" listicles used to drive substantial traffic. Now they're competing with Google's AI Overview, which often cites Wirecutter, NYT Wirecutter, Engadget, and similar sources for its named brand recommendations. If your brand isn't in those source articles, you don't get cited in the Overview, and the user clicks the cited source instead of your brand site.

### Pattern 3: brand pages slipping below the Overview

For branded queries ("Casper mattress"), Overviews often appear with a comparison-style answer (Casper vs Purple vs Saatva). The user might click any brand's site or none at all. Your branded query click-through rate erodes even though you still rank #1 for your own name.

## The recovery playbook

You can't reverse AI Overviews. You can change which side of the citation chip you're on. The recovery playbook focuses on becoming the cited source rather than the ranked-but-uncited page.

### Step 1: identify which of your pages got cited and which didn't

Google AI Overviews show citation chips. You can see them by running your top 50 commercial keywords and observing which sources Google cites.

For each query, note:
- Did your brand site get cited?
- If yes, which page?
- If no, what sources were cited instead?
- Why might Google have preferred those sources over yours?

The "why might Google have preferred" answer almost always comes down to one of three things:
- The cited source has more structured data
- The cited source has more recent publication date
- The cited source has clearer entity attribution (better headline, clearer category framing)

### Step 2: rewrite your highest-value pages to be quotable

AI Overviews paraphrase from source pages. Pages that are easy to paraphrase get cited more often. Pages that bury the answer in long marketing prose get ignored.

The "quotability rewrite" for a typical CPG page:

- Lead with a direct one-sentence answer to the implied query
- Follow with specific facts (numbers, certifications, comparisons)
- Use clear headings that match likely query intent
- Include a TL;DR or summary box near the top
- Cut filler phrases like "we believe" and "our team is passionate about"

Example before: "At Casper, we believe sleep is the foundation of a thriving life, and our award-winning mattresses are crafted with the philosophy of bringing you the most restful nights possible..."

Example after: "Casper is a direct-to-consumer mattress brand. Our Original Mattress is built with three layers of memory foam, costs $1,295 for a queen, ships free, and includes a 100-night trial. It's designed for medium-firm comfort suitable for back and side sleepers."

The "after" version is 100x more likely to be cited by an AI Overview.

### Step 3: add (or improve) Product, Organization, and FAQ schema

Covered in [our schema post](/blog/schema-markup-for-dtc-brands-2026), but worth restating in this context: AI Overviews rely heavily on structured data to decide what your page is about. Pages with proper Product schema get cited at meaningfully higher rates than pages without.

### Step 4: get into the listicles AI Overviews cite

For "best of" queries, AI Overviews often cite Wirecutter, NYT Wirecutter, Engadget, Consumer Reports, and category-specific publications. If your brand is in those source articles, you get the citation. If not, you don't.

This is PR work, not SEO work. Build relationships with editorial staff at the publications your category cites most often. Pitch them for "best of [year]" inclusions. Send products for review. Track which publications consistently appear in AI Overviews for your category and prioritize those.

### Step 5: write the comparison page Google needs

For category queries, Google AI Overviews often want to make brand comparisons but lack a single source that compares the relevant brands fairly. If you write that comparison — including your competitors honestly — you become the source Google cites.

Examples:
- "Casper vs Purple vs Saatva: a 2026 comparison" (if you're Casper)
- "Olipop vs Poppi vs Health-Ade: which prebiotic soda is right for you" (if you're Olipop)

These pages need to actually be honest, not thinly-disguised marketing for your brand. Google AI Overviews are good at detecting bias and demote sources that look promotional.

### Step 6: build category-Reddit presence

Reddit threads are some of the most-cited sources in Google AI Overviews. Brands with strong category-Reddit footprints get pulled into the Overview by virtue of being mentioned in the source threads Google cites.

## What NOT to do

A few panic moves that don't work:

- **Bidding on more PPC to "recover" lost organic clicks.** PPC click costs in commercial queries are at all-time highs. This loses money fast.
- **Generating more AI content to "feed" the Overviews.** Google's algorithms specifically demote mass-AI-generated content.
- **Removing your blog content to "prevent" Overviews from citing it.** This just hurts you across all queries.
- **Adding noindex to pages you used to rank for.** Same outcome but worse.

## How to measure recovery

If you implement the playbook above, expect to see results in this order:

- **Week 1-4**: pages rewritten with quotable content. No traffic change yet, but you've planted the seeds.
- **Month 2-3**: Google starts citing your rewritten pages in some Overviews. AI referral traffic from Google ticks up. Branded search increases slightly.
- **Month 4-6**: Overview citations stabilize. Your share of category Overviews settles at a new level. If you've also built the PR / listicle / Reddit footprint, AI traffic compounds further.
- **Month 6+**: AI Overview traffic becomes a meaningful and growing chunk of your acquisition mix. The brands that started this work earlier are already here.

Track in Google Search Console:
- Total impressions (often go UP as Overviews include you in queries you didn't rank for before)
- Click-through rate (often goes DOWN as Overviews capture some clicks, but you're aiming for fewer-but-better)
- Branded search volume (should go UP as you get cited and shoppers Google your name)

Net traffic recovery is realistic in 4-6 months if you execute. Most brands won't, which is why those who do gain meaningful market share.
